## âš ï¸ TIME TRAPS TO AVOID

### Trap 1: Perfection Paralysis
**Symptom:** Spending 15 minutes on one problem trying to get it perfect  
**Solution:** If stuck after 5 minutes, mark it and MOVE ON

### Trap 2: Expansion Obsession
**Symptom:** Expanding every factored form when not asked  
**Solution:** Only expand if problem explicitly asks for "standard form"

### Trap 3: Verification Vortex
**Symptom:** Checking same answer 3 times  
**Solution:** One quick check is enough, save deep verification for end

### Trap 4: Arithmetic Anxiety
**Symptom:** Redoing simple calculations repeatedly  
**Solution:** Trust your calculator, show your work, move on

### Trap 5: Blank Page Panic
**Symptom:** Staring at hard problem, writing nothing  
**Solution:** Write ANYTHING - the formula, the method, what you'd try. Partial credit exists!

## ðŸŽ“ PROBLEM-SPECIFIC PACING

### Completing the Square
**Target:** 3-4 minutes
- 1 min: Setup, identify b
- 1 min: Add (b/2)Â²,  factor
- 1 min: Simplify, verify
- 30 sec: Write final answer

**Speed tip:** If a = 1, skip factoring step

### Synthetic Division
**Target:** 3-4 minutes
- 30 sec: Setup (identify c, write coefficients)
- 2 min: Perform division
- 30 sec: Write quotient/remainder
- 30 sec: Answer the question asked

**Speed tip:** Double-check signs going in, avoid redo

### Graphing
**Target:** 5-6 minutes (quadratic), 8-10 (polynomial)
- 2 min: Find key features (intercepts, vertex)
- 2 min: Plot points accurately
- 1-2 min: Sketch curve smoothly
- 1 min: Label everything

**Speed tip:** Don't need 20 points - just key features!

### Word Problems
**Target:** 7-10 minutes
- 2 min: Read carefully, identify variables
- 2 min: Set up equation/model
- 3 min: Solve
- 1 min: Interpret, check reasonableness
- 1 min: Answer in context with units

**Speed tip:** Circle what question asks - answer THAT specifically

## ðŸ”¥ FINAL TIMING WISDOM

### Three Golden Rules

**Rule 1: Done beats perfect**
A completed exam with small errors > Perfect answers on half the exam

**Rule 2: Partial credit is real**
Show your work, write your setup, demonstrate your thinking = POINTS

**Rule 3: Time is your resource**
Spend it strategically. Don't donate time to problems that don't donate points.

### If Running Out of Time (Last 10 Minutes)

**Priority order:**
1. Finish problems you're 90% done with
2. Set up remaining problems (write formulas/methods)
3. Make educated guesses on multiple choice
4. Circle answers clearly
5. Do NOT leave blanks (write something!)

### The 2-Minute Warning

**When time is almost up:**
- Stop solving new problems
- Check you answered every part of multi-part questions
- Make sure work is legible
- Verify name is on exam
- Submit with confidence!
