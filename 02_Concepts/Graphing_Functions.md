# Graphing Functions
Systematic plotting and analysis

---

## Method (General)
1) Determine domain (allowable x-values)
2) Compute key points (intercepts, special values)
3) Analyze behavior (symmetry, monotonicity, asymptotes if applicable)
4) Apply known transformations from parent functions
5) Sketch/plot accurately; label intercepts and key features
6) Verify against vertical line test (function check)

---

## Linear Functions
- Use slope and intercepts for quick sketch
- Two points determine the line

## Quadratic Functions
- Vertex form y = a(x − h)^2 + k → vertex (h,k)
- Axis of symmetry x = h; direction depends on sign of a

## Radical and Rational Functions
- Determine domain restrictions
- Identify intercepts and asymptotes (vertical and possibly horizontal)

---

## Common Errors
- Plotting (y, x) instead of (x, y)
- Forgetting restrictions (holes/vertical asymptotes)
- Misapplying transformation order
